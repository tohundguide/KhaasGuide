Frequently Asked Questions

Setting up a business in Dubai can take anywhere from one to three business weeks or more, depending on the business type, required approvals, and documentation. However, some free zones offer streamlined processes that can significantly reduce setup time, allowing entrepreneurs to start their operations more quickly.

The cost of a business setup in Dubai varies depending on several factors. It includes the type of business structure opted for, business activity to be conducted, chosen jurisdiction, availed license, required visas under the business entity, and more.

Deciding between Dubai’s mainland and free zones depends on your business activities, target market, and long-term objectives. The best option varies for each entrepreneur, and our experts are here to guide you toward the ideal solution.

Annual compliance charges depend on your business activity, business model, and its necessary legal requirements to be practiced. Taxes and maintaining the books of your entity charges will be based on your business transactions and deals. Mainland company renewals start at AED 18,000, while free zone companies start from AED 5,760.

To register a company in Dubai, you need a business license such as a commercial, industrial, or professional license. These documents are issued by the Department of Economic Development (DED) or a relevant free zone authority.

No, there is no mandatory requirement to own a residence visa to avail a business license. However, to operate your business, a residence visa is required, as this document is considered vital in the UAE and related sectors of business, mainly to open a business bank account.

Opening a corporate bank account in Dubai, UAE, is straightforward if your service provider has the right expertise for business bank account assistance. Having the right point of contact for banking is vital to understand the process and make it hassle-free. You need to provide business details and valid documents to the experts. The process usually takes 2 to 4 weeks, depending on the chosen bank.

Yes, foreign investors can hold complete ownership of their businesses in Dubai for most activities in both the mainland and free zones. However, some strategic sectors still require local sponsorship.

For mainland businesses in Dubai, renting a physical office is typically mandatory to secure and maintain a trade license. In contrast, Dubai’s free zones offer greater flexibility, with options like virtual offices or flexi-desks available for many business activities. However, certain activities may still require a dedicated office space. The requirements may vary depending on the free zone and the nature of the business.
